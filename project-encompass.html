enCOMPASS — Less Energy, Smarter Living

An integrated socio-technical approach to behavioural change for energy saving, developing innovative digital tools to make energy consumption data available and understandable for different users and stakeholders.

  • 2016 – 2019

enCOMPASS implements and validates an integrated socio-technical approach to behavioural change for energy saving, by developing innovative user-friendly digital tools for making energy consumption data available and understandable for different users and stakeholders — residents, employees, pupils, building managers, utilities, and ICT providers — empowering them to collaborate to achieve energy savings and manage their energy needs in energy efficient, cost-effective, and comfort-preserving ways.

Approach

The project demonstrates how behavioural change for energy saving can be achieved through a novel integrated approach:

  • User-Centred Visualisation: Energy data from smart sensors and user-generated information is visualised in intuitive, user-friendly ways tailored to different types of stakeholders.
  • Context-Aware Recommendations: Collaborative recommendations for energy saving are provided based on the specific context of each user, including building type, climate zone, and usage patterns.
  • Adaptive Gamification: Gamified incentives, including a board/card game based on real energy consumption, engage users and transform energy savings into playful challenges with family and friends.
  • Intelligent Control: Smart metering infrastructure and home automation sensors enable automated energy management alongside user-driven behavioural change.

Pilot Studies

enCOMPASS was piloted across three European climatic zones, covering three types of buildings (residential, school, and office):

  • Haßfurt, Germany: 100 residential homes, 10 classrooms in 1 school, and 1 public building. EIPCM coordinated this pilot in collaboration with Stadtwerke Haßfurt and NABU.
  • Thessaloniki & Athens, Greece: 100 residential homes, 1 school, 1 retail store, 2 floors in an office building, and 1 public library.
  • Gambarogno, Switzerland: 100 residential homes, 1 school, and 1 public building.

EIPCM Contribution

EIPCM co-managed the project and played a key role in directing the pilot studies. The research focus was on visualisation and context-based recommendations for behavioural change, developing methods to make complex energy data accessible and actionable for diverse user groups.

Expected Impact

The enCOMPASS platform was expected to reduce energy consumption (electricity and heating) and related CO2 emissions by up to 20–25%.

Business Ecosystem

  • Platform as a Service: The enCOMPASS architecture — including smart sensors, visualisation and gamification techniques, context detection, and personalised recommenders — was deployed in a cloud infrastructure to allow easy application development by third parties.
  • New Business Models: The platform opens a new business ecosystem featuring novel business models based on behavioural change solutions for utilities and service providers.
