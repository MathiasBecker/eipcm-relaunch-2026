Research at EIPCM

Over 17 years of research in behavioral change, AI literacy, and participatory design — with measurable impact across sustainability, energy, and digital education.

The European Institute for Participatory Media (EIPCM) is a non-profit research organization based in Berlin. Since 2009, we have led and contributed to more than 15 EU- and nationally funded projects, working with over 100 partners across Europe to develop participatory systems, behavioral change platforms, and AI literacy programs.

Our work produces measurable results: 21% water savings through gamified feedback, 20% energy reductions in pilot households, 91% AI competency gains among young learners, and statistically significant improvements in user engagement through persuasive UX design. We do not just study participation — we build and rigorously test the systems that make it work.

Our Research Approach

Our research follows a consistent cycle: we start with real users, not assumptions. Through co-creation workshops, stakeholder interviews, and field observation, we identify actual needs and behaviors. We then design interventions grounded in established behavioral science frameworks — including persuasive systems design, gamification theory, and adaptive incentivization models.

Every solution is prototyped, piloted in real-world settings, and evaluated with statistical rigor. Across our projects, we have conducted hundreds of user interviews, organized co-creation initiatives with over 30.000 participants, and validated outcomes through controlled experiments. This practice-driven approach ensures our research produces not only publications but working systems with documented impact.

Core Research Themes

1. Human-Centered & Trustworthy AI

This theme investigates how people can develop critical understanding of AI systems and their societal implications. We examine "AI literacy" — the ability to understand how language models, image recognition, and generative AI function, their potential biases and failure modes, and the evidence (or lack thereof) underlying their outputs. Our research addresses a critical gap: realizing the benefits of AI while mitigating its risks requires more than regulatory frameworks — it requires equipping people with the conceptual tools to assess AI systems critically.

Our work spans multiple stakeholder groups. With journalists, we investigate how editorial workflows can incorporate AI literacy and source verification practices. With young learners, we examine how hands-on experimentation with AI systems builds competency and healthy skepticism. Our research integrates established pedagogical frameworks with interactive, playful experiences — moving beyond passive learning to active participation. Evidence from our projects shows that targeted, interactive AI literacy interventions significantly improve participant understanding and confidence in engaging with AI systems.

2. Sustainability & Behavioral Change

This theme investigates how behavior change towards sustainability can be designed and measured rigorously. We examine a critical gap: providing information about environmental impact rarely motivates sustained behavior change. Our research focuses on "designing for behavioral change" — applying established frameworks from behavioral economics, persuasive systems design, and gamification theory to create systems that actively support users in making more sustainable choices.

Key research questions include: How can real-time feedback loops reinforce conservation behavior? What role do social comparison and achievement recognition play in sustaining engagement? How can interfaces be designed to make abstract impacts (water saved, energy reduced) tangible and rewarding? Our work spans residential energy management, water conservation, and smart metering, deployed with utilities across multiple European countries and climate zones. This long-running research stream combines behavioral science with rigorous field experimentation and statistical evaluation, producing measurable reductions in resource consumption and documented improvements in user engagement and satisfaction.

3. Participatory Systems & Social Innovation

This theme investigates how digital platforms can mobilize "collective intelligence" to address societal challenges. We examine how participatory systems can connect end-users, professionals, and institutions in ways that surface creative solutions and distribute decision-making power across diverse stakeholders. Key research questions include: How can platforms be designed to make "different perspectives and knowledge created, exchanged and negotiated in networked interactions" legible and actionable? What technological and organizational structures support successful collaboration across institutional boundaries?

Our research encompasses digital social innovation platforms, knowledge visualization for collaborative analysis, and tools supporting open innovation and citizen engagement. We investigate both the technological infrastructure — semantic structuring, interactive visualization, crowdsourcing mechanisms — and the human and organizational factors that enable productive participation. This stream combines platform design, user research, and quantitative evaluation of engagement and outcomes, demonstrating how participatory systems can scale beyond traditional boundaries to mobilize communities for creative problem-solving.

4. Persuasive & Adaptive UX Design

This theme investigates how interface design and information architecture can actively support users in making better decisions — particularly in complex domains like energy management, tariff selection, and understanding technical systems. Our research examines "persuasive systems design" — the deliberate application of behavioral principles to create interfaces that influence behavior without manipulation or deception.

We explore how different user segments respond to different information structures and presentation styles. Some users benefit from detailed technical data and control options; others are better served by simplified interfaces with curated recommendations. Our work draws on established frameworks including Fogg's Behavior Model, Cialdini's principles of influence, and behavioral economics. Rather than designing a single interface for all users, we investigate how adaptive systems can recognize user preferences and tailor interface elements accordingly — balancing transparency with usability. Our research emphasizes evidence-based evaluation: testing interface variants with real users, measuring decision quality and confidence, and iterating based on rigorous behavioral metrics.

Impact Domains

Our research is applied across three primary domains:

  • Energy & Water: Behavioral change platforms for residential energy management, water conservation, smart meter integration, and intelligent tariff tools. Deployed with utilities in Germany, Switzerland, Spain, and Greece. (SmartH2O, enCOMPASS, SIT4Energy)
  • AI Literacy & Education: Interactive learning environments and coaching tools that help journalists, educators, and young people critically engage with AI systems. (AI-CODE, iKIDO)
  • Digital Social Innovation: Platforms and methodologies for crowdsourcing, citizen engagement, and collective intelligence connecting grassroots innovation with institutional support. (CHEST)
